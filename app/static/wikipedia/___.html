Ṭ

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Ṭ (minuscule: ṭ) is a letter of the Latin alphabet, formed from T with the addition of a dot below the letter. It is used in the transcription of Afro-Asiatic languages to represent an "emphatic t", and is used in the Kabyle Roman alphabet.

In former usage, it represented the retroflex T of Javanese, but this is now written as "th."
