Berteaucourt-lès-Thennes is a commune in the Somme département in the Picardie region of France.

[edit] Geography

Situated on the D935 road, close to the banks of the Somme some 9 miles southeast of Amiens. The soil around the town comprises alluvial deposits in the valley of the Luce, the small river that separates Berteaucourt from Thennes, red diluvium around the school and out towards Thézy-Glimont, chalk towards the slopes and Eocene clay on the higher ground.

[edit] History

Being very close to the front line during much of the First World War 1914-1918, the town was all but destroyed. Every building, as well as the cemetery, suffered a great deal of damage. The German attack of March 21, 1918 on the front line from Saint-Quentin to La Fère had succeeded in creating a pocket 80km deep into the combined British and French lines. General Seely set up his headquarters at Berteaucourt-lès-Thennes in March, 1918. On August 8, 1918, the French troops left Thennes and Berteaucourt-lès-Thennes, counter-attacking towards Villers-aux-Érables.
