Ishmael Welsh-Elliott (born 4 September 1987 in Deptford) is an English professional footballer. He is currently playing for Grays Athletic. His regular position is in midfield.

Welsh began his career as a trainee with West Ham United (commonly known as The Academy of Football), turning professional in July 2005. He failed to break into the first team with West Ham and was released in May 2006. In July that year he signed a two-year contract with Yeovil Town[1] and scored on his league debut on August 12, 2006 in Yeovil's 2-1 win at home to Carlisle United.[2]

Despite being in Yeovil's first team at the start of the season, Welsh struggled to establish himself and was allowed to join Weymouth on loan in March 2007.[3] He returned to Yeovil before the end of the season,[4] playing in the final league game of the season away to Gillingham, but failed to make the squad for any of Yeovil's play-off matches.

In August 2007, Welsh joined Torquay United on a season-long loan.[5] On 11 November Torquay met Yeovil in the first round of the FA Cup and Yeovil demanded that Torquay not include Welsh in the team to face them otherwise they would recall the midfielder back from his loan spell in January.[6] Despite Yeovil's warning Torquay included him on the bench for the match but he was not brought on during the game as Torquay came away with a 4-1 win. After his spell at Torquay he returned back to Yeovil, but was then quickly loaned back out, this time to Jim Harvey's Forest Green Rovers for the remainder of the 2007-08 season.[7]

On 7 May 2008, Grays Athletic announced that Welsh had signed for the Essex outfit.[8]
