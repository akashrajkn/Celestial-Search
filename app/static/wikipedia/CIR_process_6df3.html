CIR process

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

The CIR process (named after its creators John C. Cox, Jonathan E. Ingersoll, and Stephen A. Ross) is a Markov process with continuous paths defined by the following stochastic differential equation:

This process is sometimes called squared Bessel process (also see Bessel process) in the mathematical literature, and can also be defined as a sum of squared Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes. The process value at time t, i.e. rt follows a noncentral chi-square distribution. The CIR is an ergodic process, and possesses a stationary distribution, which is a gamma.

This process is widely used in finance to model short term interest rate.
