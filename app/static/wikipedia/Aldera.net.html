Aldera.net

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Aldera.net, also known as "The University of Aldera", was a largely popular Star Wars blog created shortly after the release of Star Wars, Episode I: The Phantom Menace. Throughout its two and a half year existence, Aldera.net was known for posting highly sensitive material about the upcoming Episode II and was sent an order to cease and desist by LucasFilm on at least two occasions. Contrary to popular belief, Aldera.net was not shut down by LucasFilm when it went offline in 2003.

[edit] History

Aldera.net was created in January 2000 by webmaster Charles Taylor. The blog slowly gained popularity for its insightful take on rumors about the upcoming Episode II. Aldera.net's first major boost in popularity came when it named Hayden Christensen as the new Anakin Skywalker months before it was made official.

[edit] Storyboards

Aldera.net was made famous in late September 2000 when it posted scans of an Episode II storyboard of the "rain duel" fight sequence between Obi-Wan Kenobi and Jango Fett. Within a matter of hours LucasFilm had contacted Aldera.net's ISP - Net Nation - and demanded that they remove the web site from their network. Net Nation declined to do so without a court order. Webmaster Charles Taylor removed the storyboards after speaking with LucasFilm's lawyers.

Although difficult to verify, this may have marked the first occasion a major film studio threatened legal action against a blogger.

[edit] The Script

Among the sensitive material publish on Aldera.net before the release of Star Wars Episode II was a complete outline of the secretive plot to Star Wars Episode II, including quotes of dialogue directly from the script.

[edit] Production Art

The second cease and desist order came from LucasFilm when Aldera.net posted production art of Count Dooku's star ship.

[edit] Official Spoof

A reference to Aldera.net's webmaster Charles Taylor was made on an official Star Wars web site called Holonet News (holonetnews.com). The article discusses formal censoring and suspension of The University of Aldera's Dean "Halcor Raystel" - an anagram for "Charles Taylor". The article also described it's subject as a womanizer, drug user, and a "thorn in the side."
