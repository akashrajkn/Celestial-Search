[edit] History

South Suburban School (Main) was established on 13th February in 1874. It was initially located at Hazra Pukur (now Jatin Das Park) in Kolkata. At that time it was known as South Suburban School, Kalighat. Pundit Sibnath Sastri was the first head-master of this school. After a few years the school's name was changed to South Suburban School, Bhowanipur. The school was relocated to Govinda Bose Lane i.e. its current location. The Secondary building of the school was built in 1888 by an Italian engineer and its primary building was built in 1908.

Asutosh College was established by this school. This school also has a history of helping other schools , including L.M.S. Institution (now known as South Suburban School (Branch)), Sir Ramesh Mitra Balika Vidyalaya and Chakraberia High School.

[edit] Campus

The school is located at Govinda Bose Lane.There are two buildings and each building has its own playground.Bigger one is known as 'Boro Bari' and other as 'Choto Bari'.

[edit] Uniform

The uniform of the school is a white shirt with Ass colour shorts or trousers, white socks and black shoes. Physical education days require a white trouser and white cades instead and in winter Navy Blue sweaters are allowed. There is also a school emblem i.e. 'S' that should be on the pocket of the shirt.

[edit] Activities

The school has an active NCC curriculum having Army, Air and Naval wings and students of this school also participated in Republic Day March in Delhi.

This school has a pretty good sports environment.This school encouraged its students to partcipate in various sporting event.There is a School Cricket team which regularly participates in the verious school level tournaments.
