[edit] Brazil

  • NAe São Paulo: 32,800 tons ex-French FS Foch, purchased 15 November 2000

[edit] France

  • FS Charles de Gaulle: 40,600 tons nuclear powered aircraft carrier, commissioned 18 May 2001

[edit] India

  • INS Viraat: 28,700 tons ex-British HMS Hermes, purchased 19 April 1986 and commissioned May 1987, scheduled to be decommissioned in 2012.

[edit] Being rebuilt

  • INS Vikramaditya: 45,000 tons STOBAR modified ex-Russian Admiral Gorshkov, planned to enter service in 2012.

[edit] Under Construction

  • Vikrant class carrier (Indigenous Aircraft Carrier): to be named INS Vishaal, 37.500 tons STOBAR aircraft carrier. It is being built at Cochin Shipyard, southern India, and is expected to enter service in 2012.

[edit] Italy

  • Giuseppe Garibaldi (551): 13,850 tons Italian STOVL, commissioned September 1985.
  • Conte di Cavour (550): 27,100 tons Italian STOVL, commission date 2008.

[edit] Russia

  • Admiral Flota Sovetskovo Soyuza Kuznetsov: 66,000 tons Admiral Kuznetsov class STOBAR aircraft carrier. Launched in 1985 as Tbilisi, renamed and operational from 1995.

[edit] Spain

  • Principe de Asturias: 13,400 tons STOVL carrier, commissioned 30 May 1988.

[edit] Under construction

  • Juan Carlos I: 27,000 tons Buque de Proyección Estratégica class amphibious ship/VSTOL carrier, to be commissioned in 2008.

[edit] Thailand

  • HTMS Chakri Naruebet: 11,400 tons carrier based on Spanish Principe De Asturias design. Commissioned 10 August 1997, though remains predominantly inactive due to lack of funds.

[edit] United Kingdom

  • HMS Illustrious (R06): 20,600 tons Invincible STOVL class aircraft carrier, commissioned 20 June 1982, scheduled to be decommissioned in 2012.
  • HMS Ark Royal (R07): 20,600 tons Invincible STOVL class aircraft carrier in commissioned 1 November 1985, scheduled to be decommissioned in 2015.

[edit] In Storage

  • HMS Invincible (R05): 20,600 tons Invincible STOVL class aircraft carrier, commissioned 11 July 1980 and currently in storage.

[edit] Planned

  • HMS Queen Elizabeth (CVF): 65,000 tons Royal Navy CVF programme expected in 2014.
  • HMS Prince of Wales (CVF): 65,000 tons Royal Navy CVF programme expected in 2016.

[edit] United States

  • USS Kitty Hawk (CV-63): 82,200 ton, Kitty Hawk-class conventional-powered supercarrier, in active service, commissioned 21 April 1961. Due for decommissioning in 2008.
  • USS Enterprise (CVN-65): 93,500 ton nuclear-powered supercarrier, commissioned 25 November 1961. First nuclear-powered aircraft carrier. Due for decommissioning in 2015.
  • USS Nimitz (CVN-68): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 3 May 1975.
  • USS Dwight D. Eisenhower (CVN-69): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 18 October 1977.
  • USS Carl Vinson (CVN-70): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 13 March 1982.
  • USS Theodore Roosevelt (CVN-71): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 25 October 1986.
  • USS Abraham Lincoln (CVN-72): 104,000-ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 11 November 1989
  • USS George Washington (CVN-73): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 4 July 1992.
  • USS John C. Stennis (CVN-74): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 9 December 1995.
  • USS Harry S Truman (CVN-75): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear-powered supercarrier in active service, commissioned 25 July 1998.
  • USS Ronald Reagan (CVN-76): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear powered supercarrier, in active service, commissioned 12 July 2003.

[edit] Under construction

  • USS George H. W. Bush (CVN-77): 104,000 ton Nimitz-class nuclear powered supercarrier in construction, commissioning scheduled for 2009.
  • USS Gerald R. Ford (CVN-78): 100,000 ton Ford-class nuclear powered supercarrier in design and development stage, commissioning scheduled for 2015.
