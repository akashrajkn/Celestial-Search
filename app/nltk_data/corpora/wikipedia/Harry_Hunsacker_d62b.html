Harry Hunsacker is a fictional detective in a series of stage plays that are unusual in that they are presented in "Living Black and White". This technique, developed at Pegasus Theatre, employs trade-secret makeup, lighting, costuming, and performance style to create the illusion that you are viewing onstage a black-and-white motion picture of the 1930s or 40's.

Author and star Kurt Kleinmann has been writing and performing with Pegasus Theatre since 1985 as part of the of artistic renaissance that has occurred in Deep Ellum in recent years.

Parody of 1930's/1940's detective movies.

New Year's Eve performance tradition.

[edit] Re-Occurring Characters

Harry Hunsacker: would-be actor turned would-be detective

Nigel Grouse: Hunsacker's paid-by-the-hour assistant and close personal friend

Lt. Foster: police officer

Other characters mis-pronounce Hunsacker: ("Hamsnacker", "Crumbsacker", "Hunsucker", "Humpsmacker", "Harsbacker", "Humdrummer", "Humdinger", "Hampecker", "Henpecker", "Hamhacker", Hamswinger", Hambagger", "Humswacker, "Hensmacker",etc.)

During the course of the play, a connection is made which reveals that the villainous Dr. Big is red/green colorblind and cannot distinguish the difference between two vases on the stage.)
