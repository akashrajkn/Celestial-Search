[edit] Barton Street Station

Barton Street police station is one of three London Borough of Canley police stations, along with Sun Hill and Spicer Street. Sgt. Doug Wright is one of the sergeants at Barton Street, who worked alongside his wife, Sgt. Nikki Wright. After Sgt. June Ackland retires from active service, Sgt. Nikki Wright transfers to Sun Hill. Commander Ian Barrett is stationed at Barton Street, which is the Borough Headquarters. There is often a rivalry between Barton Street and Sun Hill.

[edit] Department of Professional Standards (DPS)

A recurring DPS DCI is Frank Keane, father of Sun Hill PC, Emma Keane. The DPS has been called in on many an occasion to investigate officers accused of corruption; PC Emma Keane, DI Neil Manson and DCI Jack Meadows have all been interviewed by DPS.

[edit] Central Operations Nineteen (CO19)

Formerly known as Special Operations 19 (SO19), CO19 is the Armed Response unit for the Metropolitan Police. Sgt. Mark Rowlins is a CO19 sergeant who is the partner of the PC Lance Powell. Between 2001 and 2003, PC Dale Smith transfers to CO19 and is involved with an accidental shooting of Sgt. Bob Cryer. A few years later, a promoted Dale Smith returns to Sun Hill.

[edit] Crown Prosecution Service (CPS)

Sun Hill has a CPS office in the station to aid officers with matters involving the judiciary system. Previous CPS Lawyers at Sun Hill have included Jonathan Fox and Matt Hinckley.
