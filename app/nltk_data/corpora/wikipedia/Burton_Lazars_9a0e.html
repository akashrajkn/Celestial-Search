Burton Lazars

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Burton Lazars parish church

Burton Lazars is a small village 2 miles south-east of Melton Mowbray in Leicestershire.The name originates from Burton Saint Lazarus after Lord Roger de Mowbray opened a Leper Hospital to take advantage of the natural spring water in the 12th century.

The village currently has population of between 400-500.

The Order of Saint Lazarus established leper hospitals with the first being in Jerusulem in 530 ad . The English branch of the order was based at Burton Lazars, Leicestershire in 1150 ad. It was suppresed by Henry VIII in 1544.
