Antonio Francesco Orioli

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Antonio Francesco Orioli O.F.M.Conv. (born 10 December 1778 in Bagnacavallo, Faenza in Italy, died 20 February 1852 in Rome) was a cardinal of the Catholic Church.

Orioli joined the Order of the Friars Minor Conventuals (a branch of the Franciscans). He professed on May 6, 1793 at the convent of Bologna. He was educated at the Franciscan colleges of Bologna and Parma, and St. Bonaventure College in Rome. In 1804 he received a doctorate in arts and theology.

From 1807 to 1809, Orioli was Lector of theology at St. Bonaventure College in Rome. He moved to France in 1809, returning to Italy in 1812. Here he became a professor at St. Bonaventure College in 1817.

He was also definitor general of his order and consultor of the S.C. of the Index. He was an examiner of the Roman Clergy for the promotion to academic chairs. On September 4, 1832, he became vicar general of his order.

On April 15, 1833, Orioli was elected bishop of Orvieto, being consecrated by Cardinal Giacinto Placido Zurla on May 1 of that year. He served at the basilica of Ss. XII Apostoli in Rome. Cardinal Zurla was assisted by Giovanni Soglia, titular archbishop of Efeso, and by Alessandro Bernetti, bishop of Recanati.

He was made cardinal in February 1838 by Pope Gregory XVI. He received the red hat and the title of S. Maria sopra Minerva, February 15, 1838. In December of 1841, he resigned pastoral government of the diocese. He participated in the conclave of 1846 to elect Pope Pius IX. Orioli became prefect of the S.C. of Bishops and Regulars on May 2, 1847. From May 5 to June 4 of 1848, he served as Secretary of State ad interim.

He opted for the title of Ss. XII Apostoli, September 30, 1850.

Orioli died in Rome. He was exposed and buried in the church of Ss. XII Apostoli. Pope Pius IX participated in his funeral.
